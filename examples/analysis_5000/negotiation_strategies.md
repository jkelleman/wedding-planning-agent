# Negotiation Strategies: Reducing Prix Fixe Options

## Strategy Overview

**Goal:** Negotiate lower pricing by offering fewer menu choices  
**Rationale:** Fewer options = reduced kitchen complexity, less waste, easier service coordination

---

##  Target Scenarios

### Scenario 1: The Banks Dinner Tier 2
**Current Offering:**
- Price: $115 per person ($2,875 for 25 guests)
- Standard: 4-5 entree selections

**Proposed Negotiation:**
- Price: $105 per person ($2,625 for 25 guests)
- Offer: 3 entree selections only
- **Savings: $250 total (9% reduction)**

**Justification:**
"We would like to simplify the menu to 3 entree selections instead of your standard 4-5 options in Tier 2. This would reduce kitchen complexity while still providing excellent variety for our 25 guests. We're happy to work with you on which entrees work best for your team."

---

### Why Reducing Options Works

**From Venue's Perspective:**
1. **Inventory Management:** Fewer ingredients to order and manage
2. **Kitchen Efficiency:** Streamlined prep reduces labor costs
3. **Food Waste:** Less risk of preparing dishes that aren't selected
4. **Service Speed:** Faster plating and coordination
5. **Quality Control:** Easier to perfect fewer dishes

**From Your Perspective:**
1. **Lower cost** without sacrificing quality
2. **Simplified guest choices** (paradox of choice)
3. **Faster service** on event day
4. **Still provides variety** for dietary needs

---

### Expected Outcomes

**Realistic Expectations:**
- **5-10% discount:** Most likely scenario
- **10-15% discount:** Possible with good negotiation and timing
- **15%+ discount:** Rare, usually only in off-season

**Alternative Value-Adds:**
If they won't reduce base price, ask for:
- Complimentary champagne toast
- Upgraded dessert option
- Extended cocktail hour
- Waived room fee or cake cutting fee
- Wine pairings included

---

### Conversation Starters

**In-Person/Phone:**
- "We love your venue! Is there flexibility on pricing if we simplify the menu?"
- "What if we reduced the entree choices - could that affect the per-person cost?"
- "We're comparing several options. Can you work with us on customizing a package?"
- "Is there a tier between [X] and [Y] that offers fewer selections?"

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
‚ùå "Your competitor offers this cheaper"
‚ùå "This seems really expensive"
‚ùå Demanding or entitled tone
‚ùå Threatening to go elsewhere (unless true)

##  Negotiation Checklist

Before reaching out:
- [ ] Know your absolute maximum budget
- [ ] Research the venue's competitors  
- [ ] Have 2-3 backup venue options
- [ ] Identify your preferred date (and backup dates)
- [ ] Know which menu simplifications you're proposing
- [ ] Prepare your justification clearly

During negotiation:
- [ ] Be polite and professional
- [ ] Emphasize excitement about the venue
- [ ] Explain your budget constraint honestly
- [ ] Propose specific menu modifications
- [ ] Show flexibility on details
- [ ] Ask about alternative value-adds
- [ ] Get any agreements in writing

After negotiation:
- [ ] Document what was agreed
- [ ] Request updated proposal in writing
- [ ] Compare final offers from all venues
- [ ] Make decision within stated timeframe
- [ ] Send thank you note to venues (even if declined)
