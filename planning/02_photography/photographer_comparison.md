## üìã What to Ask Photographers

### Initial Inquiry Questions:
1. Are you available on [your date]?
2. What's your pricing for a 3-hour event covering 3 locations (hotel, BPL, restaurant)?
3. Do you have experience shooting at the Boston Public Library?
4. Have you photographed at The Banks or similar Boston restaurants?
5. What's included in your packages?
   - Number of edited photos delivered
   - Turnaround time
   - Digital rights
   - Online gallery
   - Print rights
6. Can we see full galleries from similar multi-location intimate events?
7. What's your photography style? (Documentary, posed, mix)
8. Do you charge travel fees between locations in the same area?
9. Do you offer payment plans?
10. What's your backup plan if you're unable to shoot?
11. Can we schedule a call or meeting to discuss?

### Follow-Up Questions:
12. Do you have liability insurance? (Required by BPL for commercial photography)
13. What's your cancellation/postponement policy?
14. Will you be the photographer on the day, or an associate?
15. How many photos can we expect from 3 hours across 3 locations?
16. Do you offer add-ons (engagement session, prints, albums)?
17. What's your editing timeline?
18. Are you comfortable navigating between three locations in Back Bay?
19. Do you need any special permits for BPL photography?
20. What's your approach to weather contingencies?

##  Photography Style Guide

**Documentary/Photojournalistic:**
- Candid, natural moments
- Minimal posing
- Story-driven
- **Best for:** Authentic, real moments

**Editorial/Fine Art:**
- Artistic composition
- Magazine-quality
- More dramatic
- **Best for:** Fashion-forward, modern aesthetic

**Classic/Traditional:**
- Timeless portraits
- Mix of posed and candid
- Elegant, refined
- **Best for:** Traditional, formal events

**Modern/Contemporary:**
- Clean, bright editing
- Natural light focused
- Lifestyle approach
- **Best for:** Casual, relaxed events

##  Money-Saving Tips

1. **Book during off-peak season** (Nov-Mar = better rates)
2. **Weekday events** often cost 20-30% less
3. **Shorter coverage** = lower cost (3 hrs vs 8 hrs)
4. **Skip engagement session** if not needed
5. **Digital-only package** (no prints/albums)
6. **Book associate photographer** at studios (Nicole Chan: $5,500 vs $7,000)
7. **Negotiate custom package** between elopement and wedding pricing
8. **Ask about last-minute availability** (discounts possible)
