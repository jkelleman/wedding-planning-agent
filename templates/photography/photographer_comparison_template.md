##  Evaluation Criteria

Use this checklist when evaluating photographers:

### Style & Portfolio
- [ ] Portfolio matches your aesthetic preference
- [ ] Examples from similar events (elopement, traditional, outdoor, etc.)
- [ ] Examples from your venue or similar venues
- [ ] Editing style you love (light & airy, moody, vibrant, classic)
- [ ] Mix of candid and posed shots in portfolio
- [ ] Detail shots well-represented

### Experience & Professionalism
- [ ] [#]+ years experience
- [ ] Shot [#]+ weddings
- [ ] Has backup equipment
- [ ] Has backup photographer policy
- [ ] Carries liability insurance
- [ ] Provides contract
- [ ] Professional communication
- [ ] Reviews and references available

### Packages & Pricing
- [ ] Within your budget
- [ ] Clear package details
- [ ] Number of hours appropriate
- [ ] Engagement session included (if desired)
- [ ] Second shooter included (if needed)
- [ ] Digital images included
- [ ] High-resolution downloads
- [ ] Print release provided
- [ ] Album option available
- [ ] Timeline for delivery (typically 6-8 weeks)

### Logistics
- [ ] Available on your date
- [ ] Willing to travel to your venue
- [ ] Familiar with your venue or willing to scout
- [ ] Flexible with timeline
- [ ] Comfortable shooting in your lighting conditions
- [ ] Has permit requirements handled (if shooting in public spaces)

### Personal Fit
- [ ] Easy to communicate with
- [ ] Understands your vision
- [ ] Personality match (important - you'll spend hours together!)
- [ ] Makes you feel comfortable in front of camera
- [ ] Flexible and accommodating
- [ ] Enthusiastic about your event

##  Photography Selection Tips

### Research Phase
1. **Start early** - Book 6-12 months out for popular photographers
2. **Look beyond price** - Cheapest isn't always best value
3. **Review FULL weddings** - Don't just look at highlight reels
4. **Check recent work** - Style and quality should be consistent
5. **Read reviews** - Look for professionalism, communication, delivery

### Consultation Phase
1. **Meet before booking** - Video call or in-person
2. **Ask to see full wedding gallery** - Not just highlights
3. **Discuss your vision** - Make sure they understand
4. **Review contract carefully** - Understand cancellation, delivery, rights
5. **Trust your gut** - Personal connection matters

### Questions to Ask
- How many weddings have you photographed?
- Do you have backup equipment?
- What happens if you're sick on my wedding day?
- When will I receive my photos?
- How many edited images should I expect?
- Do I get high-resolution files?
- Can I print the photos myself?
- Do you shoot RAW? Will I receive RAW files?
- What's your editing style?
- Have you shot at my venue before?
- Do you have liability insurance?
- What's your payment schedule?
- What's your cancellation/refund policy?

### Red Flags to Watch For
-  No contract provided
-  Requires full payment upfront
-  No backup equipment or plan
-  Inconsistent portfolio quality
-  Poor communication/slow to respond
-  No reviews or references
-  Unwilling to show full wedding galleries
-  Unclear about deliverables
-  Extremely low pricing (too good to be true)
