##  Important Questions to Ask

Customize this list based on vendor category:

### For All Vendors:
1. Are you available on [date]?
2. Have you worked at [venue] before?
3. How many [events] have you done?
4. What's included in the package price?
5. What are the add-on costs?
6. What's your payment schedule?
7. What's your cancellation/refund policy?
8. Do you have liability insurance?
9. What's your backup plan if there's an emergency?
10. Can I see references or a full portfolio?
11. What's the timeline for [deliverables]?
12. How do you handle [specific concern]?

### Category-Specific Questions:

**For Photographers/Videographers:**
- How many edited photos/video will I receive?
- What's your editing style?
- Do I get high-resolution files?
- Is there a second shooter?
- When will I receive final products?

**For Venues/Caterers:**
- What's the food/beverage minimum?
- What's included in rental (tables, chairs, linens)?
- How do you handle dietary restrictions?
- What's the service staff ratio?
- Are there any restrictions (music, decor, timing)?

**For Florists:**
- Can you work within seasonal availability?
- What's your experience with [specific flowers]?
- Do you provide setup/breakdown?
- What happens if flowers aren't available?
- Can I see photos of similar arrangements?

**For DJs/Musicians:**
- What equipment do you provide?
- Do you take requests from guests?
- How do you handle announcements/MCing?
- What's your music library size?
- Can you learn specific songs?

##  Vendor Comparison Tips

### Research Phase
1. **Cast a wide net** - Contact 5-10 vendors initially
2. **Use multiple sources** - Google, social media, The Knot, WeddingWire, local wedding groups
3. **Look beyond price** - Cheapest isn't always best value
4. **Check recent work** - Styles and quality can evolve
5. **Read full reviews** - Not just star ratings

### Evaluation Phase
1. **Create consistent criteria** - Use same questions for all vendors
2. **Take good notes** - Easy to forget details after meeting multiple vendors
3. **Trust your gut** - Personality fit matters
4. **Consider communication** - How quickly/professionally do they respond?
5. **Look for red flags** - Pushy sales tactics, vague answers, inconsistent information

### Decision Phase
1. **Sleep on it** - Don't book immediately after consultation
2. **Compare total costs** - Not just base package prices
3. **Reread contracts carefully** - Understand what you're committing to
4. **Negotiate when appropriate** - Many vendors have flexibility
5. **Book with confidence** - Once decided, don't second-guess

### Red Flags to Watch For
-  No written contract
-  Requires full payment upfront
-  No insurance or backup plan
-  Inconsistent portfolio quality
-  Poor or slow communication
-  No reviews or refuses to provide references
-  Extremely low pricing (too good to be true)
-  Pushy or aggressive sales tactics
-  Vague about deliverables or timeline
-  Bad reviews about similar issues
